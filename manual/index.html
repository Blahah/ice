Introduction

The Joint BioEnergy Institute's Inventory of Composable Elements (ICE) is an open source registry software and platform for managing information about synthetic biological parts. It provides support for microbial strains, plasmids, Arabidopsis seeds and generic parts. Within the ICE system these are generally referred to as Entries.

Login

To log into ICE, enter your username and password. If you have forgotten your password, click on the "Forgot your password?" link, enter your username and click Reset your password. This will reset your password and a temporary password will be emailed to you.

Usernames are typically your email address.
You may create a user account by clicking on "Create Account" and providing the information requested. A temporary password will be generated and emailed to you on successful account creation.
If the "Create Account" button is not available then it implies that the registry instance does not permit user registrations and might be integrated with another user directory system. In that case contact the administrator of the site.

Adding Entries

You will be able to add entries to ICE once you are logged in. There are two ways to add entries in ICE:

  • Using the single entry form
  • Using the bulk upload interface to upload multiple entries at the same time

Creating single entries

Click on create entry and select the type of entry to add. Fill out the information and upload the sequence information and click submit. The form is validated to ensure that the required information is entered before the record is created.

Uploading multiple entries

Click on create entry and select the type of entry to add. Fill out the information and upload the sequence information and click submit. The form is validated to ensure that the required information is entered before the record is created.
